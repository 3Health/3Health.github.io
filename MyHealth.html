Current Methods of Patient Education are Overwhelming

One of the key issues experienced by patients with chronic disease, especially those recently diagnosed, is patient education on their illness and how best to manage it. When patients first go to specialist appointments they face an overwhelming amount of information about their condition and the potential treatment options that are available to them. They are not able to learn and retain all the important information presented to them. Therefore, a lot of education needs to take place outside the office and at the patientsâ€™ own pace. Currently, physicians have to rely on recommending online resources and pamphlets, but the information these resources provide is not personalized and is still overwhelming.
